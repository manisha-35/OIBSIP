  • Healthy human foods are typically nutrient-dense, meaning they provide a wide range of essential nutrients while being relatively low in unhealthy substances like added sugars, saturated fats, and artificial additives.

    Remember, portion control is important, and a balanced diet that incorporates a variety of these foods is key to maintaining good health.

  • There is no specific amount of junk food that can be considered "good" for consumption because it's not a beneficial food group for overall health. However, occasional indulgence or moderation can be practiced.

  • 'Healthy living' means maintaining a healthy lifestyle and introducing habits that improve your health. It can be difficult to change old habits, but there are steps you can take to become healthier. An important first step is identifying less healthy habits and learning new, positive ones to replace them, such as:

    eating healthy foods and balanced meals ,sleeping well and managing stress taking responsibility for your overall health including having regular check-ups for your eyes and teeth.

  • Healthy meals consist of a combination of nutrient-dense foods that provide a balance of carbohydrates, proteins, healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals.

  • To maintain adequate hydration, it is generally recommended to drink about 8 cups (64 ounces) of water per day. However, individual water needs can vary based on factors such as activity level, climate, and overall health. It's important to listen to your body's thirst signals and drink water throughout the day to stay properly hydrated.
    It's worth noting that water can be obtained not only through drinking plain water but also from other beverages and water-rich foods like fruits and vegetables.
